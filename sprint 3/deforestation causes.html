Main Causes of Deforestation

Deforestation occurs when forests are cleared for agriculture, mining, or urban expansion. Farmers often cut trees to make space for crops or cattle grazing, while industries harvest timber for paper, furniture, and construction. In some regions, illegal logging and wildfires accelerate forest loss. These actions reduce the number of trees that absorb carbon dioxide and protect biodiversity.
