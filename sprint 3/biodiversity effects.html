Major Effects of Biodiversity Loss

When biodiversity declines, ecosystems become unstable, human livelihoods suffer, and the planet‚Äôs resilience to change weakens.

1. Ecosystem Imbalance

The extinction of species disrupts food chains, water cycles, and pollination systems essential to life.

2. Reduced Food Security

Fewer species mean fewer crops and fish stocks for human consumption, increasing hunger and poverty.

3. Loss of Natural Medicine Sources

Many modern medicines come from plants and animals found in biodiverse ecosystems. Their loss limits future medical discoveries.
