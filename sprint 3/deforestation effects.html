Major Effects of Deforestation

Deforestation has far-reaching effects on the planet‚Äôs climate, biodiversity, and human life. The destruction of forests disrupts the delicate balance of ecosystems.

1. Climate Change

Trees absorb carbon dioxide; cutting them down releases it back into the atmosphere, accelerating global warming and altering weather patterns.

2. Loss of Biodiversity

Forests are home to over 80% of terrestrial species. When forests disappear, animals lose habitats and face extinction.

3. Soil Erosion and Desertification

Tree roots hold soil together. Without them, rain washes fertile soil away, reducing land productivity and leading to desertification.
