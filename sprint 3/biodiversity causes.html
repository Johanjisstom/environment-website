Main Causes of Biodiversity Loss

Biodiversity loss occurs when species disappear or ecosystems degrade due to human and environmental pressures. It threatens the balance of life on Earth.

1. Habitat Destruction

Deforestation, mining, and urban expansion destroy natural habitats, leaving species without homes or food sources.

2. Pollution

Chemicals, plastics, and waste harm animals and plants by contaminating their air, soil, and water environments.

3. Climate Change

Rising global temperatures alter habitats and migration patterns, pushing many species toward extinction.
